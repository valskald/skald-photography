Statement, process, and ways to connect.

Artist Statement

I work where fine art and street photography meet. I’m drawn to material culture and the evidence of touch—objects, thresholds, and the way light cuts across ordinary spaces. Low-light scenes—lamps, windows, silhouettes—let comfort and unease coexist. I don’t aim for ambiguity; interpretation is part of viewing. These images are records of encounter, shaped by interests in nostalgia, possessions, semiotics, and the histories they carry.

Process

Walks at dusk and night. Workflows focus on restraint in editing—contrast, subtle color shifts, careful crop.

Ethics

Street work respects privacy and context. I avoid sensationalism, foregrounding place, object, and line.

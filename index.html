About

I photograph when a subject’s color, line, or light–shadow structure demands attention. My practice sits between fine art and street photography and is informed by training in history, libraries, and archives.

I focus on material culture and human interaction with objects, alongside the ways nature appears in everyday spaces. Recurring motifs include thresholds and low light—lamps, windows, silhouettes—where comfort and unease coexist. I don’t aim for ambiguity; interpretation is simply part of viewing. The work functions as a record of encounter, shaped by interests in nostalgia, personal possessions, semiotics, and the histories they carry.
